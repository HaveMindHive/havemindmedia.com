Pythagoras

c. 570 BC — c. 495 BC
△
Harmonic Ratios

"All is Number."

Founded a school where mathematics was mysticism. Discovered that musical harmony follows exact mathematical ratios. Believed the cosmos itself was a harmony of numbers — the "Music of the Spheres."

⚔ School attacked, scattered. Likely murdered or starved in exile.

Plato

c. 428 BC — c. 348 BC
⬡
Platonic Solids / Forms

"Let no one ignorant of geometry enter here."

Above the Academy door. Five perfect solids — tetrahedron, cube, octahedron, dodecahedron, icosahedron — as the building blocks of reality. The physical world as shadow of geometric Forms.

✓ Died respected. Ideas later suppressed by Church.
κ Connection

Platonic solids emerge from κ. Tetrahedron = 4×(π/3). All five from angular ratios.

Hypatia of Alexandria

c. 355 AD — 415 AD
◯
Conic Sections

"Reserve your right to think, for even to think wrongly is better than not to think at all."

Last great mathematician of Alexandria. Edited Apollonius's work on conic sections — ellipse, parabola, hyperbola. These curves describe ALL orbital motion. She saw it. The Church could not allow a woman to hold such knowledge.

⚔ Dragged from her chariot, stripped, flayed with oyster shells, burned.
κ Connection

Conic sections = κ slicing through the cone of observation. All orbits are κ curves.
