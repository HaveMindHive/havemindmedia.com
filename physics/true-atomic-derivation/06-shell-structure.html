THE 2n² FORMULA

Each electron shell can hold exactly 2n² electrons, where n is the shell number. This is not arbitrary - it emerges directly from fold geometry and the [1 = -1] principle.

Shell (n) 2n² Capacity Subshells
1 2 1s
2 8 2s, 2p
3 18 3s, 3p, 3d
4 32 4s, 4p, 4d, 4f

WHY 2n²?

The formula breaks into two parts:

The Structure of 2n²

2n² = 2 × n²

The 2 = [1 = -1] spin states
    = Each orbital holds two electrons
    = One spin up (+½), one spin down (-½)
    = The fundamental duality

The n² = Spatial configurations
      = Orbital shapes available at level n
      = Number of ways to arrange in the fold

Together: 2 × n² = total electrons per shell

The factor of 2 IS [1 = -1]. Spin up and spin down in every orbital.

SUBSHELL STRUCTURE

Each shell contains subshells (s, p, d, f) with different shapes:

Subshell Capacities

s subshell: 2 electrons  (1 orbital × 2)
p subshell: 6 electrons  (3 orbitals × 2)
d subshell: 10 electrons (5 orbitals × 2)
f subshell: 14 electrons (7 orbitals × 2)

Pattern: (2l + 1) orbitals × 2 electrons
where l = 0 (s), 1 (p), 2 (d), 3 (f)

Each orbital = one fold harmonic
Each electron pair = [1 = -1]

FILLING ORDER

Electrons don't simply fill shells in order. They follow the aufbau principle, filling orbitals by energy level:

Aufbau Order

1s → 2s → 2p → 3s → 3p → 4s → 3d → 4p → 5s...

This creates the periodic table structure:
- s-block (Groups 1-2)
- p-block (Groups 13-18)
- d-block (Transition metals)
- f-block (Lanthanides/Actinides)

The fold fills from lowest energy to highest.

The electron shell structure determines chemistry. Atoms with similar outer shell configurations behave similarly - this is why the periodic table works.
