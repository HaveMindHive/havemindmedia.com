You discovered it!

When a point stays the same distance from a center and walks everywhere it can... it makes a circle.

What Is a Circle, Really?

Most people think a circle is just a round shape. But that's like saying a song is "just sounds."

A circle is something much more interesting:

A circle is everywhere a point can reach while staying the same distance from center.

That "same distance" has a special name: the radius.

Try This:

Tie a string to a pencil. Hold the other end in place. Now draw with the pencil, keeping the string tight. What shape do you make?

The Circle Keeps a Secret

The point on the circle is always moving. But something stays the same.

Watch the distance from center. Does it ever change?

Distance from Center
1.00

The point can be anywhere on the circle — top, bottom, left, right — but the distance to center is always the same.

The Circle's Secret

Everything on a circle is equally connected to the center.

Why Does This Matter?

Circles are everywhere:

  • ⊙ Wheels roll because every point is the same distance from center
  • ⊙ Planets orbit in (nearly) circles around stars
  • ⊙ Sound waves, light waves — they're circles too (we'll see how!)
  • ⊙ The ripples when you drop a stone in water

When you understand the circle, you start to understand everything that moves.

The circle isn't just a shape.
It's the shape of motion.

What You Learned

  • ✓ A circle is all the points the same distance from center
  • ✓ That distance is called the radius
  • ✓ The circle is the shape of motion and connection
