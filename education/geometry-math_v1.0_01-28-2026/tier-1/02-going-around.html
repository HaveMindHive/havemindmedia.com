One Full Trip = One Rotation

When the point travels all the way around the circle and returns to where it started, that's called one rotation.

A rotation has a number

One full rotation = 360 degrees (or 360°)

Why 360? Ancient astronomers noticed the sun takes about 360 days to complete its yearly cycle. So they divided the circle into 360 equal parts.

The Big Idea

When the point goes all the way around, it ends up exactly where it started.

This is called periodicity. Things repeat. Cycles complete. What goes around, comes around.

Where do we see this?

• Day and night (Earth rotating)

• The seasons (Earth orbiting the sun)

• Your heartbeat (a repeating rhythm)

• Music (sound waves repeating)

The circle is the shape of cycles.
And cycles are everywhere.
