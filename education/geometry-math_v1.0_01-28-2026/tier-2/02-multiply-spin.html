Tier 2 - Connection

Multiplication is Rotation

What if multiplying numbers was really about spinning in a circle?

The Standard View

In school, you learned: 2 × 3 = 6

"Two groups of three" or "three groups of two." That's one way to think about it.

But there's another way — a geometric way — that reveals something deeper.

Numbers on the Number Line

Imagine numbers as positions on a line. Zero is in the middle. Positive numbers stretch right. Negative numbers stretch left.

Now, what happens when you multiply by -1?

Multiplying by -1 flips to the opposite side.
That's a 180° rotation!

Negative as Rotation

Start at 1

1

Position: right

Multiply by -1

1 × (-1) = -1

Rotate 180°

Multiply again

-1 × (-1) = 1

Rotate 180° more = back to start!

Two half-rotations = one full rotation = you're back where you started.

This is why (-1) × (-1) = 1. It's not a rule to memorize — it's geometry!

But What Rotates 90°?

If -1 is a 180° rotation, what number would give a 90° rotation?

Think about it: if you rotate 90° twice, you get 180°. So this mystery number, multiplied by itself, should equal -1.

Enter: i (the imaginary unit)

i × i = -1

The number i is defined as the square root of -1.
But geometrically, it's simply: a 90° rotation.

The Deep Insight

When you multiply complex numbers:

  • Magnitudes multiply — scaling
  • Angles add — rotation

Multiplication = Rotation + Scaling. That's it. All of complex number arithmetic is just spinning and stretching on a plane.

Multiplication isn't about "repeated addition."
It's about rotating and scaling in space.

What You Learned

  • ✓ Multiplying by -1 is a 180° rotation
  • ✓ The number i is a 90° rotation
  • ✓ i × i = -1 because 90° + 90° = 180°
  • ✓ Complex multiplication = rotation + scaling
