The Ancients Asked

"If the inscription at Delphi had read 'SEE THYSELF' instead of 'KNOW THYSELF' — how would one accomplish this?"
— Socrates, in Plato's Alcibiades I (c. 390 BCE)

Socrates answered: look into the pupil of another's eye.

The Greek word for pupil is κορη (kore) — meaning "doll" or "little image."

You see yourself as a tiny figure in another's eye. The eye IS a mirror.

The Atomists' Answer

"These images are like a skin, or a film, peeled from the body's surface, and they fly this way and that across the air."
— Lucretius, De Rerum Natura, Book IV (c. 55 BCE)

Epicurus and Lucretius proposed that all objects constantly shed simulacra — thin films of atoms streaming outward at incredible speed.

The mirror proves this theory.

No matter how quickly you move, the mirror instantly reflects. Because simulacra are always streaming. The mirror simply redirects the stream.
