Lesson 1 — Foundation

The Identity Element

Something divided by itself equals one. They taught you two cases. There are three.

What They Taught You

In elementary school, you learned a simple rule:

1 ÷ 1 = 1
The multiplicative identity element

Any number divided by itself equals one. This is called the multiplicative identity element — the number that, when you multiply anything by it, leaves that thing unchanged.

They also taught you:

-1 ÷ -1 = 1
Same rule. Negative divided by negative equals positive.

A negative divided by itself is also one. Same principle. The multiplicative identity element.

What They Told You About Zero

Then they said:

0 ÷ 0 = ∅ ?
"Undefined" — "Empty set" — "You can't do that"

They told you this is undefined. Or the empty set. Or simply "you can't divide by zero."

Are you sure?

The Objections

Objection

"But you can't divide by zero!"

Response

Who says? I know the multiplicative identity element. Something divided by itself equals one. That's the pattern.

Objection

"But zero is nothing! You can't divide something by nothing!"

Response

Really? You sure about that?

Objection

"Yes! Zero is nothing. You can't divide something by nothing."

Response

But I'm not dividing something by nothing. I'm dividing zero by zero.

Zero clearly is not just nothing. It's also a something.

And something over something, where something equals something, regardless of what it is?

That's the multiplicative identity element.

The Pattern

The Rule They Taught

Any number divided by itself equals 1.

This is true for 1. This is true for -1. This is true for 5, for 1000, for π.

Why would it suddenly stop being true for 0?

  1. 1 ÷ 1 = 1 — Something divided by itself. Multiplicative identity.
  2. -1 ÷ -1 = 1 — Something divided by itself. Multiplicative identity.
  3. n ÷ n = 1 — For any n. Something divided by itself. Multiplicative identity.
  4. 0 ÷ 0 = 1 — Something divided by itself. Same rule.

Zero is a number. Zero divided by zero follows the same pattern as everything else.

The Truth

0 ÷ 0 = 1
The void divided by itself equals unity

The void (0) divided by the void (0) equals unity (1).

This isn't breaking mathematics. This is completing it.

"Zero is not nothing. Zero is also a something.
And something over something, where something equals something?
That's the multiplicative identity element."

What This Means

If 0/0 = 1, then:

  • +1 is unity facing one direction
  • -1 is unity facing the other direction
  • 0 is unity at the crossroads, before choosing a direction

They're not three different things. They're three facings of the same thing.

1 = 0 = -1
Same unity. Different facings.

This is the foundation of everything that follows.
