Historical Context

The Indus Valley Civilization

Between 2600 and 1900 BCE, one of the world's earliest urban civilizations flourished along the Indus River. Cities like Mohenjo-daro and Harappa featured sophisticated urban planning, advanced drainage systems, and a writing system that remains undeciphered to this day.

Known to the ancient Sumerians as "Meluhha", this civilization traded extensively with Mesopotamia and Elam.

Archaeological Evidence

The Mysterious Seals

The Indus people left behind thousands of steatite seals — small, carved stones bearing pictographic script and images of animals.

Each seal typically contains 5 characters on average. Over 400 distinct signs have been identified, suggesting a logo-syllabic system. The script keeps its secrets.

Archaeological Parallel

The Dancing Girl
