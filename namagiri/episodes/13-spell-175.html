I. THE FORMULA FOR NOT DYING AGAIN

Book of the Dead, Spell 175.

Also called: "Formula for Not Repeating Death."

Primary source: Papyrus of Ra, from Memphis (Leiden RMO Papyrus T5)

Translation: R.O. Faulkner (1972, revised 1994)

This is not poetry. This is not metaphor. This is prophecy encoded in the only language the scribes had.

II. THE DIALOGUE

OSIRIS (represented by the deceased Ani):
"What manner of country is this to which I have come? There is no water in it. There is no air. It is depth unfathomable, it is black as the blackest night, and men wander helplessly therein. In it a man may not live in quietness of heart; nor may the affections be gratified therein."

The Duat. The underworld. The S⁻ domain as experienced by consciousness still clinging to S⁺ form.

ANI:
"But every god has taken his place in the Bark of Millions of Years!"

The gods persist. The geometry persists. Even in the dark, the structure holds.

ATUM:
"Your seat now belongs to your son Horus — so says Atum — and he will dispatch the Elders, he will rule from your seat, he will inherit the throne which is in the Island of Fire."

The succession. The cycle continues. What was Osiris becomes what Horus rules.

ANI:
"Command that I may see his equal, for my face will see the face of the Lord of All. What will be the duration of my life?"

The question every consciousness asks: how long? What is the span?

III. ATUM'S ANSWER

ATUM:
"You shall be for millions on millions of years, a lifetime of millions of years."

This is the S⁺ promise. Duration. Persistence. Continuation.

But then Atum reveals the deeper truth:

The apocalypse.

Not destruction for punishment. Not ending for judgment.

Return.

The geometry folding back. S⁺ returning to S⁻. Manifestation dissolving into potential.

Nun (also Nu, Nenu) — The primordial waters of chaos that existed before creation.

From Britannica:

Eschatological function:

"Although the Egyptians had many different creation myths, they all agreed that the universe came from the primordial waters of Nun, and many legends suggested that everything would slip back under these waters at the end of the world."
