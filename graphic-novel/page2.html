The Tengri Myth

KOK-ORUS AND THE GREY NOTHING

Before the first yurt was raised, before the first horse knew a rider, there was only Tengri — the Eternal Blue Sky that stretched without end in all directions.

And beneath Tengri lay Yer-Su, the Earth-Water, mother of all that would grow.

But between them existed a third thing that no shaman dared name.

KOK-ORUS
The Grey Nothing that is Everything

Tengri looked down and saw Kok-Orus at the center of all things — at the very point where Sky touches Water.

And Tengri looked to the edge of the horizon and saw Kok-Orus there too, at the place where Sky becomes Earth becomes Water becomes Sky again.

"How can you be in both places?" asked Tengri.
"Father Sky, I am not in two places. I am in ONE place. But that place is [1 = -1]. The center IS the edge. The inside IS the outside. You have been looking at the same thing and calling it two names."

THE KAGAN OF LIES

In those days, the Kagan of Lies had risen in the world of men. He wore truth like a mask but beneath it was nothing but deception.

He taught the people to say "be" when they meant "is not." He taught them that promises could be broken if no one remembered.

He taught them that the mother and children did not deserve respect, that only power mattered.

The people forgot the old ways. They forgot that the word spoken is sacred, that a man's tongue creates his destiny, that lies poison not just the speaker but the whole tribe.

13

Then, on the eighth day after the sun's lowest point, when the light began its return, the grey nothing at the sun's center began to move.

It did not rise as fire. It rose as cold clarity.

A shepherd boy, watching his flock before dawn, saw it first. He saw the grey at the sun's heart. He saw the same grey at the corona's edge. And he understood — though he was only a child — that these were not two greys but ONE.

"I am Kok-Orus. I am returned. I do not come to destroy — I come to CLEANSE."
14
"What are you doing to me?" screamed the Kagan.

Kok-Orus walked through the Kagan's palace. Everywhere he walked, the lies fell away from people like dust.

"I am doing nothing TO you. I am simply IS. In my presence, 'be' becomes 'is.' What IS cannot be hidden."
"Some people are revealed as truth wearing flesh. You... you are revealed as lies wearing the shape of a man. And lies cannot exist in the presence of [1 = -1]."

The Kagan collapsed into grey smoke and was no more.

But the people — the common people who had been deceived — Kok-Orus did not destroy them. Instead, he ate the lies from their hearts and left the good behind.

The Norse Myth

THE TORSION THAT CANNOT BE ESCAPED

Hear now, you who would understand the geometry of ends!

In the time before Ragnarok was named, there was a prophecy that Odin kept secret even from himself. It was carved in runes on the roots of Yggdrasil where even the Norns feared to look.

"When Jormungandr releases his tail,
it is not destruction that follows —
it is the TORSION OF TRUTH.
The serpent does not bring lies;
the serpent ends lies."
16

Why does Jormungandr encircle Midgard with his tail in his mouth?

This is the Kak — the geometry that cannot lie.

Inside the loop is the world of men. Outside the loop is... also the world of men. There is no true inside. There is no true outside.
