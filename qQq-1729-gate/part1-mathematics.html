One complete rotation divided into 12 equal steps

2. Torsion Fields

2.1 What is Torsion?

In the 1729 Sphere OS, torsion (τ) represents rotational force or "twist." Unlike linear force that pushes or pulls in a direction, torsion creates rotation around a center.

Think of wringing a towel: your hands apply torsion in opposite directions. The towel doesn't fly away — it twists. In our system, torsion is the fundamental force that drives the wheel's rotation.

2.2 The Torsion Law

The most important equation in the entire system is the Torsion Balance Law:

τ₁ + τ₂ + τ₃ + τ₄ = 0
The sum of all torsion forces always equals zero

This is not an approximation — it's a hard constraint. No matter what inputs the system receives, the total torsion must balance to zero. This mirrors the physical principle that angular momentum is conserved in a closed system.

  • Manifest nodes (diagonal): NW, NE, SW, SE — These receive user input
  • Shadow nodes (cardinal): N, S, E, W — These are computed automatically

The shadow nodes are not free variables — they're computed as the negative of the manifest sum, distributed equally among the four shadow positions:

"No, Hardy, it's a very interesting number. It's the smallest number expressible as the sum of two cubes in two different ways."
