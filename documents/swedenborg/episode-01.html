The Five Comparative Definitions

Swedenborg examined the Infinite under five aspects, comparing it systematically to the finite:

Infinite vs. Finite

  • 1. Expanse — Infinite is UNBOUNDED; Finite is BOUNDED
  • 2. Origin — Infinite is SELF-EXISTENT; Finite is DERIVED from prior
  • 3. Duration — Infinite is ETERNAL; Finite is TEMPORAL
  • 4. Substance — Infinite is CONTINUOUS unity; Finite is DISCRETE particles
  • 5. Motion — Infinite is FRICTIONLESS; Finite circles, bounds

The first four definitions present an impossible gap. How can the unbounded create bounds? How can the self-existent create the derived? How can eternity produce time? How can continuity produce discreteness?

Only under the fifth definition — MOTION — can the Infinite accommodate to the finite without destroying itself.

The Finiting of Infinity

Swedenborg's solution: the Infinite finites itself through vortical motion. Motion creates boundaries without violating the nature of the Infinite, because motion is a mode of being, not a substance.

"The first finiting of Infinity is in the nature of vortex rings small as points, produced in the substance of the Infinite."
— Principia Rerum Naturalium (1734)

These "natural points" are not mathematical points (which have no dimension) nor physical particles (which have fixed substance). They are motion itself — spiral whirls in the infinite medium that create apparent boundaries without breaking continuity.

"In space without space — space was not until lower finites came into existence."
— Principia

This is the key insight: the natural points are not IN space — they CREATE space by their rotation. Space itself emerges from the finiting process, not as a pre-existing container.

The Series of Finites

From the first natural point, Swedenborg derives a series of finites — each emerging from the previous through further degrees of motion:

The Descent from Infinity

  • First Finite — The natural point. Vortex ring in infinite substance.
  • Second Finite — Aggregation of first finites. Greater complexity.
  • Third Finite — The "ether" or first medium of transmission.
  • Fourth Finite — Magnetic element. Force fields emerge.
  • Fifth Finite — The material world as we perceive it.
"All things which involve an end, constitute a circle."
— Principia

This is the principle: motion that has a purpose (an "end") must return to itself. The infinite creates circles — bounded forms — by curving back upon itself. Each finite is a circle within a larger circle, all the way back to the Infinite.
