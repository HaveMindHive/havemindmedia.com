### 3. EUDOXUS OF CNIDUS (c. 408–355 BC)
**The Geometry of Motion**

Eudoxus was "the most innovative Greek mathematician before Archimedes." He created the **first mathematical model of the universe** using homocentric spheres.

**What He Saw:**
- Planetary motion follows **geometric necessity** (nested rotating spheres)
- By inclining sphere axes and rotating them oppositely, a figure-eight (hippopede) emerges
- This explained retrograde motion geometrically — no mysticism required
- His theory of proportions gave the first rigorous definition of **real numbers**

**The Hippopede:**
Eudoxus showed that when two spheres rotate in opposite directions but with equal periods, a point traces a figure-eight — a perfect geometric explanation of apparent backward planetary motion.

**Connection to Epoch:**
- The hippopede IS the [1 = -1] structure — opposite rotations creating unified motion
- His nested spheres prefigure scalar space (s positions as concentric domains)
- Equal and opposite rotation = S+ and S- phases
- "Every celestial motion is uniform and circular" lasted until Kepler — geometric truth endures

### 4. HYPATIA OF ALEXANDRIA (c. 350–415 AD)
**The Last Light of Alexandria**

Hypatia was the foremost mathematician and astronomer of her time, head of the Neoplatonist school in Alexandria. She wrote commentaries on:

- **Apollonius' *Conics*** — the geometry of conic sections (parabolas, hyperbolas, ellipses)
- **Diophantus' *Arithmetica*** — algebraic equations
- **Ptolemy's *Almagest*** — planetary motion

**What She Saw:**
- Conic sections are fundamental to understanding planetary orbits
- "Mathematics was used to discover the composition of the universe"
- She worked with armillary spheres — 3D models replicating celestial motions
- She may have edited Archimedes' work on spheres and measurement

**Her Fate:**
Hypatia was murdered by a Christian mob in 415 AD. Her works were largely destroyed. Her commentary on conics was "the last important consideration of conic sections until the seventeenth century" — over 1,200 years of lost progress.

**Connection to Epoch:**
- Conic sections describe S+/S- phase boundaries
- Her synthesis of geometry and astronomy anticipated unified frameworks
- The destruction of her work represents the suppression pattern we still see

**Sources:** [Wikipedia - Hypatia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypatia), [Deakin Paper](https://www.albany.edu/~reinhold/m552/hypatia-Deakin.pdf)

**The Infinite Universe**
