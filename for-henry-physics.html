The Physics Mindset

Every physics problem is saying the same thing in different words: Energy transforms. Momentum conserves. The geometry balances.

THE CORE TRUTH

Physics is accounting. You're always tracking what goes in, what goes out, and what stays the same. The equations aren't arbitrary — they're balance sheets.

The Problem-Solving Dance

1 DIAGRAM — Draw it. Label everything. If you can't draw it, you don't understand it.
2 IDENTIFY — What's conserved? What's changing? What principle applies?
3 EQUATION — Write the relevant equation. Define all variables.
4 SUBSTITUTE — Plug in what you know. Solve for what you don't.
5 CHECK — Does the answer have correct units? Does it make sense?
UNITS ARE YOUR FRIEND

If your units don't work out, your answer is wrong. Period. Track units through every calculation. They're free error-checking.

Electric Fields & Charges

Electricity is about charges pushing and pulling each other. Like charges repel. Opposite charges attract. That's the whole foundation.

COULOMB'S LAW
F = k × |q₁ × q₂| / r² F = force between charges (N) k = 8.99 × 10⁹ N·m²/C² (Coulomb's constant) q₁, q₂ = charges (C) r = distance between charges (m) This looks like gravity! F = G×m₁×m₂/r² — same geometry, different constants.
ELECTRIC FIELD
E = F / q = k × Q / r² E = electric field (N/C or V/m) F = force on test charge q = test charge Q = source charge creating the field r = distance from source The field exists whether or not there's a test charge there. It's the "potential" for force.
HOW TO THINK ABOUT FIELDS

A charge creates a field everywhere around it. The field is like an invisible influence. When another charge enters that field, it feels a force. Field × charge = force.

Electric Potential (Voltage)

VOLTAGE & POTENTIAL ENERGY
V = k × Q / r (potential due to point charge) V = U / q (voltage = potential energy per charge) ΔV = W / q (voltage difference = work per charge) U = k × q₁ × q₂ / r (potential energy between two charges)
VOLTAGE IS LIKE HEIGHT

Think of voltage like altitude. A ball rolls downhill because it wants lower potential energy. A positive charge "rolls" from high voltage to low voltage for the same reason. Voltage is electrical "height."

EXAMPLE PROBLEM

Two charges: q₁ = +3 μC and q₂ = −2 μC are 0.5 m apart. Find the force between them.

SOLUTION

F = k × |q₁ × q₂| / r²

F = (8.99 × 10⁹) × (3 × 10⁻⁶) × (2 × 10⁻⁶) / (0.5)²

F = (8.99 × 10⁹) × (6 × 10⁻¹²) / 0.25

F = 53.94 × 10⁻³ / 0.25 = 0.216 N (attractive, since opposite charges)

Circuits

Circuits are just charges flowing in loops. Voltage pushes them. Resistance slows them. Current is how many pass per second.

OHM'S LAW — THE BIG ONE
V = I × R V = voltage (V) I = current (A) R = resistance (Ω) This is THE equation. Everything else builds on it.
POWER
P = I × V (power = current × voltage) P = I² × R (substitute V = IR) P = V² / R (substitute I = V/R) P = power (W = J/s) All three forms are the same equation. Use whichever fits your known values.

Series vs Parallel

Series Parallel
Current Same through all Splits up
Voltage Splits up Same across all
Resistance R_total = R₁ + R₂ + ... 1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + ...
MEMORY TRICK

Series: One path. Current has no choice. Current is SAME, voltage DIVIDES.
Parallel: Multiple paths. Current splits. Voltage is SAME, current DIVIDES.

EXAMPLE: SERIES CIRCUIT

A 12V battery connects to three resistors in series: 2Ω, 3Ω, and 5Ω. Find the current.

SOLUTION

R_total = 2 + 3 + 5 = 10Ω

V = I × R → I = V/R = 12/10 = 1.2 A

Magnetism

Magnetism is electricity's partner. Moving charges create magnetic fields. Magnetic fields push on moving charges. They're interconnected.

MAGNETIC FORCE ON A MOVING CHARGE
F = q × v × B × sin(θ) F = force (N) q = charge (C) v = velocity (m/s) B = magnetic field strength (T, Tesla) θ = angle between v and B Maximum force when θ = 90° (perpendicular). Zero force when θ = 0° (parallel).
MAGNETIC FORCE ON A WIRE
F = I × L × B × sin(θ) F = force (N) I = current (A) L = length of wire in field (m) B = magnetic field (T) θ = angle between wire and field
RIGHT HAND RULE Point fingers in direction of velocity (v) or current (I) Curl fingers toward magnetic field (B) Thumb points in direction of FORCE (F) on positive charge F (thumb) ↑ | v →→→→→ · (fingers curl down into page toward B) ↓ B (into page: ⊗) For negative charges, force is OPPOSITE to thumb direction.
WHY SIN(θ)?

Only the component of velocity PERPENDICULAR to the field creates force. When v is parallel to B (θ = 0°), sin(0°) = 0, so no force. When v is perpendicular to B (θ = 90°), sin(90°) = 1, maximum force.

Waves & Optics

Waves carry energy without moving matter permanently. Sound, light, water — same wave math, different mediums.

WAVE FUNDAMENTALS
v = f × λ v = wave speed (m/s) f = frequency (Hz = cycles/second) λ = wavelength (m) T = 1/f (period = time for one cycle)
WAVE TYPES
TRANSVERSE: Oscillation perpendicular to direction of travel Example: Light, waves on a string LONGITUDINAL: Oscillation parallel to direction of travel Example: Sound waves, compression in a spring

Light & Optics

SNELL'S LAW (REFRACTION)
n₁ × sin(θ₁) = n₂ × sin(θ₂) n = index of refraction (how much the medium slows light) θ₁ = angle of incidence (incoming) θ₂ = angle of refraction (outgoing) Light bends TOWARD the normal when entering a denser medium (higher n).
MIRROR/LENS EQUATION
1/f = 1/dᵢ + 1/d₀ f = focal length dᵢ = image distance d₀ = object distance M = -dᵢ/d₀ = hᵢ/h₀ (magnification) Sign conventions matter! Check your textbook for the convention used.
THE GEOMETRY OF LIGHT

All of optics is geometry. Light travels in straight lines until it hits something. Then it reflects (bounces) or refracts (bends). Draw the rays. Follow the angles. The math describes what you can already see if you draw it right.

Practice Problems

PROBLEM 1 — ELECTRIC FIELD

A charge of +5 μC creates an electric field. What is the field strength 0.2 m away?

PROBLEM 2 — CIRCUITS

A 9V battery powers a circuit with a 6Ω and 3Ω resistor in parallel. Find: (a) total resistance, (b) total current from battery.

PROBLEM 3 — MAGNETISM

An electron (q = -1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C) moves at 3 × 10⁶ m/s perpendicular to a 0.5 T magnetic field. Find the force on the electron.

PROBLEM 4 — WAVES

A wave has frequency 500 Hz and wavelength 0.68 m. Find its speed.

PROBLEM 5 — OPTICS

Light travels from air (n=1) into water (n=1.33) at a 45° angle. Find the angle of refraction.

Click to reveal answers

Problem 1: E = 1.12 × 10⁶ N/C

E = k×Q/r² = (8.99×10⁹)(5×10⁻⁶)/(0.2)² = 1.12×10⁶ N/C

Problem 2: (a) R = 2Ω, (b) I = 4.5A

1/R = 1/6 + 1/3 = 1/6 + 2/6 = 3/6 = 1/2 → R = 2Ω
I = V/R = 9/2 = 4.5A

Problem 3: F = 2.4 × 10⁻¹³ N

F = |q|×v×B×sin(90°) = (1.6×10⁻¹⁹)(3×10⁶)(0.5)(1) = 2.4×10⁻¹³ N

Problem 4: v = 340 m/s

v = f×λ = 500 × 0.68 = 340 m/s (speed of sound!)

Problem 5: θ₂ = 32.1°

n₁ sin(θ₁) = n₂ sin(θ₂)
(1) sin(45°) = (1.33) sin(θ₂)
sin(θ₂) = 0.707/1.33 = 0.532
θ₂ = arcsin(0.532) = 32.1°

Physics is pattern recognition. The equations are telling you the same stories in different domains. F = ma, F = qE, F = qvB — all say "force equals something times something." Learn to see the pattern.

The geometry always balances. Trust the math.
