Unit 1: Fluids

PRESSURE
P = F / A P = pressure (Pa = N/m²) F = force (N) A = area (m²)
PRESSURE WITH DEPTH
P = P₀ + ρgh P₀ = atmospheric pressure (101,325 Pa) ρ = fluid density (kg/m³) g = 9.8 m/s² h = depth (m)
PASCAL'S PRINCIPLE (HYDRAULICS)
F₁/A₁ = F₂/A₂ Pressure transmits equally through fluid. Small force on small area → Large force on large area.
BUOYANCY (ARCHIMEDES)
F_b = ρ_fluid × V_displaced × g Object floats when F_b ≥ weight. Displaced volume = submerged volume.
CONTINUITY EQUATION
A₁v₁ = A₂v₂ What flows in must flow out. Narrow pipe → faster flow.
BERNOULLI'S EQUATION
P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant Faster flow → lower pressure. Explains lift, curve balls, spray bottles.
FLUIDS IN ONE SENTENCE

Pressure pushes equally in all directions. Flow conserves mass. Faster flow = lower pressure.

Unit 2: Thermodynamics

TEMPERATURE CONVERSIONS
K = °C + 273.15 °F = (9/5)°C + 32 Kelvin = absolute scale (0 K = no thermal motion)
HEAT TRANSFER
Q = mcΔT (heating/cooling) Q = mL (phase change) Q = heat energy (J) m = mass (kg) c = specific heat capacity (J/kg·K) ΔT = temperature change L = latent heat (J/kg)
IDEAL GAS LAW
PV = nRT or PV = Nk_BT P = pressure (Pa) V = volume (m³) n = moles R = 8.314 J/mol·K (gas constant) T = temperature (K) N = number of molecules k_B = 1.38 × 10⁻²³ J/K (Boltzmann)
LAWS OF THERMODYNAMICS
0th: Thermal equilibrium is transitive (If A=B and B=C, then A=C) 1st: ΔU = Q - W (Energy conservation: internal energy changes by heat added minus work done) 2nd: ΔS ≥ 0 (Entropy always increases in isolated systems)

Unit 3: Electric Force, Field & Potential

Electricity is about charges pushing and pulling each other. Like charges repel. Opposite charges attract. That's the whole foundation.

COULOMB'S LAW
F = k × |q₁ × q₂| / r² F = force between charges (N) k = 8.99 × 10⁹ N·m²/C² q₁, q₂ = charges (C) r = distance between charges (m) Same geometry as gravity: F = Gm₁m₂/r²
ELECTRIC FIELD
E = F / q = k × Q / r² E = electric field (N/C or V/m) F = force on test charge q = test charge Q = source charge creating the field
ELECTRIC POTENTIAL (VOLTAGE)
V = k × Q / r (potential from point charge) V = U / q (voltage = energy per charge) ΔV = W / q (voltage diff = work per charge) U = k × q₁ × q₂ / r (potential energy between charges)
FIELD LINES ARE S⁺ AND S⁻

Electric field lines point outward from + (S⁺) and inward to − (S⁻). The field is the [1=−1] bridge between charges.

Unit 4: Electric Circuits

Circuits are just charges flowing in loops. Voltage pushes them. Resistance slows them. Current is how many pass per second.

OHM'S LAW — THE BIG ONE
V = I × R V = voltage (V) I = current (A) R = resistance (Ω) THIS is THE equation. Everything else builds on it.
POWER — THREE FORMS
P = I × V (power = current × voltage) P = I² × R (substitute V = IR) P = V² / R (substitute I = V/R) P = power (W = J/s) All three are the same equation. Use whichever fits your knowns.
RESISTANCE
R = ρ × L / A ρ = resistivity (Ω·m) L = length A = cross-sectional area Longer wire → more resistance Thicker wire → less resistance

Series vs Parallel

Series Parallel
Current Same through all Splits up
Voltage Splits up Same across all
Resistance R = R₁ + R₂ + ... 1/R = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + ...
MEMORY TRICK

Series: One path. Current has no choice. Current SAME, voltage DIVIDES.
Parallel: Multiple paths. Current splits. Voltage SAME, current DIVIDES.

Unit 5: Magnetism & Induction

Magnetism is electricity's partner. Moving charges create magnetic fields. Magnetic fields push on moving charges. They're interconnected.

MAGNETIC FORCE ON MOVING CHARGE
F = qvB sin(θ) F = force (N) q = charge (C) v = velocity (m/s) B = magnetic field (T, Tesla) θ = angle between v and B Max force when θ=90°. Zero force when θ=0°.
MAGNETIC FORCE ON A WIRE
F = BIL sin(θ) I = current (A) L = length of wire in field (m)
MAGNETIC FIELD FROM A WIRE
B = μ₀I / (2πr) μ₀ = 4π × 10⁻⁷ T·m/A (permeability of free space) I = current (A) r = distance from wire (m)
MAGNETIC FLUX & FARADAY'S LAW
Φ = BA cos(θ) (flux in Webers) ε = -dΦ/dt (induced EMF) Changing flux induces voltage. This is how generators work!
